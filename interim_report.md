African Climate Trend Analysis – Interim Report
1. Executive Summary

Recent analyses show that Africa is warming faster than the global average, with significant increases in land and ocean temperatures over the last three decades. Rising temperatures, erratic rainfall, and more frequent extreme events such as droughts, floods, and heat waves are affecting food, water, energy security, and public health.

2. Background and Objectives

Africa’s climate-sensitive sectors are highly exposed to variability and long-term change. This study aims to:

Analyze temperature and precipitation trends
Assess climate extremes
Identify regional risks
Propose adaptation measures
3. Key Findings
Temperature Trends
Africa is experiencing rapid warming since the 1990s
East Africa shows strong temperature increases
Heat waves are becoming more frequent
Rainfall Trends
Highly variable rainfall across regions
Sahel and East Africa show extreme wet/dry cycles
Southern Africa faces increasing drought conditions
Climate Extremes
Over 110 million people affected by climate disasters in 2022
Floods and droughts cause major displacement and damage
4. Regional Risks
Horn of Africa: drought + food insecurity
West Africa: heat waves + crop stress
Southern Africa: long-term droughts
Coastal areas: flooding + sea temperature rise
5. Emerging Challenges
Unpredictable rainfall patterns
Increased food insecurity
Water scarcity
Infrastructure stress
6. Conclusion (Interim)

Africa is facing accelerating climate risks. Immediate adaptation and improved climate monitoring systems are essential to reduce vulnerability.
